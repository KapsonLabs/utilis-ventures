Democratizing Finance

Using decentralised financial solutions to build a more inclusive economy

More Info

Why should we democratise finance?

The inacessibility to affordable and reliable financial services in the developing world is a major force of growing inequality, and without sustainable interventions the cycles of dependency and insecurity will only get worse.

That is why we at Utilis Ventures are determined to fight poverty and grow wealth in emerging economies by bringing profitable financial services to more people.

How are we democratising finance?

By using technologies particularly blockchain as well as and AI, cloud computing, and electronic payments, we are using decentralised financial principles to create product offerings that empower the emerging markets to participate in the growing global economy by overcoming the hurdles of cost, trust, and location that often exclude many from accessing financial services

We believe that the transparency and trust of distributed ledger technology, the optimizing capacity of AI, power of cloud computing and global reach of electronic payments, give us an opportunity to build the cornerstones of a truly inclusive financial services ecosystem.

What are we doing to democratise finance?

Over the next several months we at Utilis Ventures will be pioneering innovative investment opportunities and decentralised financial solutions designed to demonstrate where and how value can be harnessed for the good of everyone willing to take part in the global economy.

Our Vision

Our vision is to create a future where affordable, dependable and trusted financial services are accessible to the masses; giving them not just good value for their money, but also the services and tools they need to build better futures for themselves and their communities.

We seek to create profit whilst maintaining our sense of accountability and duty as champions of a new financial age for the developing world .

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything blockchain to the details of the STO

What is Decentralised Finance?

DeFi for short is simply a financial ecosystem designed to take advantage of public blockchains technology enabling investors, entrepreneurs, businesses and individuals to deal directly with each other. Its goal is to make financial services more accessible to everyone.

What is a Blockchain?

A Blockchain is a distributed ledger, which simply means that a record of all transactions is shared across the network among all peers(members) in the network, and each peer holds a copy of the complete ledger. This ledger is often secured with cryptography and recorded chronologically this and other attributes make a blockchain very secure and transparent.

What is a blockchain wallet?

A blockchain wallet is a digital address that holds a public key for others to transfer cryptocurrency to your address and the private key so you can securely access your own digital assets

What is a Token?

Tokens are a digital asset that is often sold to the public by a company during a Security Token Offering or Initial Coin Offering campaign, usually for other cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum. Unlike other cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum, tokens do not have their own blockchain but rather are hosted on another Blockchain e.g Ethereum. A token’s use is determined by the issuing company.

What is a Security Token Offering?

Security Token Offering is a blockchain-enabled fundraising model, but it operates based on certain legal principles. Security Token Offering is also known as STO and a security token is a cryptographic token, designed to be backed by real-world tangible assets, providing a source of liquidity to the issuing company, and promising investors some sort of profit for buying and or holding the tokens.

What is the Blockchain/ DeFi revolution?

Blockchain is a disruptive technology because of its ability to safeguard personal information while offering transparency, reduce intermediaries and associated costs and time for international transactions, unlock digital assets and untapped markets, and potentially open up the global economy to hundreds of millions more participants. This has sparked the development of financial services that not only cater to those underserved by traditional financial institutions such as banks, but also provide better and more tailored services to the market at a favourable cost.
